前言

Raymond Siew 設計的 eTimeline為研究聖經歷史及其與世界歷史的相互作用提供了有用的工具。

最近，我從參加一個查經聚會中得知，耶穌在世時，是在中國的漢朝，王莽領導革命推翻國王，自己成為皇帝的時候。那很有趣。導師能夠將耶穌與中國歷史上的一個重要事件聯繫起來。這無疑提高了學生的興趣和好奇心。為了驗證這是否屬實，我迅速轉向這 eTimeline，發現這兩事件在時間上確實非常吻合。這 eTimeline為我節省了大量時間來尋找可以滿足我好奇心的答案。從那時起，它引發了進一步的興趣，因為我現在可以使用 eTimeline將聖經事件與中國歷史事件進行比較，並嘗試在它們之間尋找意義。

eTimeline非常易於使用，還有教程提供給初學者入門。它的設計，包括一個非常人性化的界面，將為許多聖經學生帶來非常獨特的體驗。這個 eTimeline拓寬了我們的視野。聖經歷史不再是一個神秘和在想像中的獨立故事，但現在可以與世界各地在現實生活中發生的事情聯繫起來。它在一個共同的時間尺度上按時間順序排列相關事件，從而節省了在紙上重新創建相同事件的時間和精力。它肯定會增強一些人對聖經的學習，並增加其他人對聖經的有效性和真實性的認知。它還展示了上帝超越世界歷史和至高無上的無可爭辯性。這讓不相信上帝的人睜開了眼睛，相信上帝是歷史的和世界的創造者。

不用說，eTimeline當然有它的限制。比如説，聖經中沒有提供許多人物和事件相關的確切年月日，有許多事件的持續時間也不是十分明顯，或者是完全沒有提及。此外，不僅在不同的種族和文化之間，甚至在同一國家的不同帝王和朝代之間，計算時間的方法也不同。這些複雜性使 eTimeline的構建變得更加困難和具有挑戰性。我認為 eTimeline肯定不能保證每事件或時間實例的準確性和正確性，但作者在這些和許多其他限制下已經盡了最大努力開發了這個獨特的“工具”。

eTimeline肯定有很大的擴展和改進空間。這種可能性僅受我們的想像力的限制。這是一件不斷進步和進化的優秀產品。在這方面，我提出了一些建議，包括：調協好挪亞方舟與一些可追溯到數千年前的古代文明的時間、加上早期教會之父，基督教在歐洲的演變，宗教改革，歷代傳教士的工作，以及過去200年來的各種屬靈運動、宗派和復興的發展。

我高度讚賞作者的聰明才智和勞力，首先是受到啟發，然後勇敢地開始並完成了這樣一個困難和具有挑戰性的項目，成為研究聖經的一項非常有用的工具。

陳建安牧師
2021年12月7日

Foreword

Raymond Siew’s eTimeline provides a helpful tool in the study of Biblical history and its relationships and interactions with other world histories.

Recently, I learned in a Bible Study that Jesus was on earth it was during the Han Dynasty in China. More specifically, it was about the time when 王莽 (Wang Mang) led a revolution to overthrow the king and became emperor himself. That was interesting. The instructor was able to relate Jesus to an important event in the Chinese history. This information has certainly increased the level of interest and curiosity. To verify if this is true, I quickly turned to the eTimeline (History section) and found that the two events indeed coincided in time. The eTimeline saved me a tremendous amount of time to find the answer that could satisfy my curiosity. Since then, it has sparked further interests since by using the eTimeline, now I can compare Biblical events with Chinese historical events, and explore meaning and significance between them.

The eTimeline is very easy to use and does not require a tutorial to initiate the beginners. Its design, including a very user-friendly interface, brings a unique experience to many students of the Bible. The eTimeline broadens our mind and allows us to see that biblical history is no longer a stand-alone story of mysteries and imagination anymore, but can be associated with what’s happening around the world in the reality of life. It chronologically aligns events on a common time scale, hence saving time and effort to re-create the same on paper. It certainly facilitates the study of Biblical events for some and increases the appreciation of the validity and reality of the Bible for others. It also presents an indisputable picture of the supremacy of God over and above the history of the world. This opens the eyes of non-believers that God is the Creator and Maker of History.

Needless to say, there are limitations. For example, the precise year, month, and day related to many characters and events are not provided in the Bible. The duration of many events is either not obvious from the Biblical text, or just totally absent. Furthermore, the method of accounting for time was different not only among diverse races and cultures, but also among dynasties and reigns under different kings and emperors in the same country. These complications make the construction of the eTimeline even more difficult and challenging. I think the eTimeline certainly cannot claim accuracy and correctness for the position of every event on the timeline, but the author has developed this unique ‘tool’ with the best effort under these and many other constraints.

There is certainly room for the eTimeline to expand and improve. The possibility is only limited by our imagination. It’s a piece of excellent work that’s in perpetual progress and evolution. In this respect, I’ve made suggestions for future editions of the eTimeline to include, for example, the:
- Reconciliation of the timing of Noah’s Ark with some ancient civilizations dated back thousands of years
- Fathers of the early churches
- Evolution of Christianity in Europe
- Missionaries over the ages, and the various spiritual movements, denominations, and revivals in the last two hundred years.

I highly commend the author’s ingenuity and dedication, first to be inspired, then to courageously embark on such a difficult and challenging project which has turned out to be a tremendously useful tool for the studying of the Bible.

Rev. Gerald K. Chan
December 7, 2021
